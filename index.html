LoungeLink Design Gallery

Priority Pass gives you lounge access but zero live information. LoungeLink is a concept for a real-time airport lounge companion — live capacity, menus, waitlists, and solo traveler matching. Below are 25 iterations exploring this idea from wild directions to a refined final version.

Brian Song 25 Iterations HTML + CSS
Phase 1 — Exploration (v1–v10)

I started by pushing the concept in 10 radically different visual directions — dark tech startup, calm editorial, brutalist manifesto, luxury first-class, retro travel poster, cyberpunk terminal, Gen-Z social app, clean SaaS dashboard, warm Airbnb-inspired, and a dark mode map app. The goal was breadth: what does a lounge companion app look like through completely different lenses? By v7, I started adding real functionality (expandable cards, seat counts, menus). By v10, I had a terminal map with travel times.

Phase 2 — Narrowing Down (v11–v19)

Two palettes stood out: v4's luxury gold-on-black and v5's warm parchment travel poster. I merged each with the full feature set (map, waitlist, profiles, menus) to compare. The warm parchment won — it felt more approachable and travel-friendly. From v15 onward, I refined: fixing map positioning, adding waitlist-only-when-full logic, splitting profile to its own page, building the 3-step waitlist flow (join → confirm → live tracker), and adding Activity and Nearby tabs.

Phase 3 — Final Refinement (v20–v25)

The last stretch was about content richness and polish. I added amenity icons, access info (hours, accepted cards, day passes), and interior lounge photos. Menus expanded to 13 items per lounge split into Food, Drinks, and Desserts with real photography. Star ratings, "Popular" and "Chef's Pick" badges, and live timestamps completed the picture. V20–V25 all share the same warm parchment palette and 5-tab structure — each iteration is a small refinement on the last.
